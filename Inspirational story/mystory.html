My family is one of the many factors that contribute to my anxiety. Yes, you read that correctly, my family, particularly my parents. My anxiety worsened when I was twice lost a scholarship due to the pandemic. I had no idea what I was going to do or where I was going. I just cried every night and told myself that I could still study. I don't want to fail because I want to show my parents that I can succeed even if they don't support me. I can't unable to open up with my parents because I believe they cannot understand me. Everything had lost its interest for me.


I simply want to get lost or go somewhere where no one knows me. I'm losing interest too in the sports that have been a part of my life and I think that is the worst part. Politics made it worse because I lost so many friends over political differences. The surrounding is full of chaos and anger as if there was no more light. I care a lot for the people around me so I get hurt easily too. Nonetheless, I allowed myself to cry since I knew it would help me feel better. Then I remind myself that no matter what happens, I will continue to get up. I also communicate with my only true best friends and share my life experiences. For them, life is brief, therefore let us not waste it in depression and sadness. Leave the rooms and go outside. Let us help ourselves because you only have yourself at the end of the day."If failing is the reason why I am strong today, I am proudly to say that I failed but not a failure".
